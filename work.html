01

Positioning & messaging

SLG Prudent AI

How I rebuilt Prudent AI's website positioning — from persona-led messaging to use-case-led narratives.

Context

Prudent AI builds AI-powered mortgage decisioning for QM and Non-QM lenders. Bootstrapped, 120+ employees, 50+ enterprise customers. Competes against Ocrolus, Laminr, and Friday Harbor.

The old homepage had three problems: it targeted a single persona (TPOs) and missed every other buyer segment, had no mention of a USP or differentiator above the fold, and showed product UI without context — no outcome framing, no proof points.

The site technically had everything a B2B homepage is supposed to have. But the people reading it were confused.

Before After

Diagnosed the gap

Audited existing messaging, analyzed how Ocrolus, Laminr, and Friday Harbor positioned themselves, and mapped buyer intent signals from real sales conversations. The homepage was speaking to one persona in a market where four different buyer types were landing on it.

Rewrote the narrative

Shifted from persona-led ("Empower your TPOs") to use-case-led ("Know if your borrower can repay before the file goes downstream"). The new headline focuses on an outcome that matters to both QM and Non-QM lenders — not a role.

Rebuilt the page architecture

Re-architected the information hierarchy. Added social proof from top lenders at the hero level. Shipped use-case landing pages mapped to active sales plays for QM and Non-QM segments.

02

Pipeline generation

SLG Prudent AI

How I capitalized on Prudent AI's existing brand in the mortgage space and built a demand gen engine to expand into new markets.

Context

When I joined Prudent AI, the company had strong brand awareness — 50+ enterprise customers, a solid reputation with Non-QM lenders, and a product that people in the space knew about. What it didn't have was a demand gen engine.

No LinkedIn ads, no outbound sequences, no landing pages. Pipeline was under $65K a quarter, driven entirely by referrals and founder-led outreach. The brand was there. The system to turn that brand into pipeline wasn't.

Phase 1

Demand gen

Capitalized on existing brand awareness. Built target account lists for QM and Non-QM lenders. Wrote LinkedIn ad copy with use-case messaging — leveraging the credibility Prudent AI already had in the space. Created dedicated landing pages for each segment. Leads that showed intent went to sales for meetings — I owned everything up to that handoff.

Phase 2 (in progress)

ABM

Now layering in account-based marketing. Researching specific target accounts, mapping buyer personas within each, writing tailored email sequences and building personalized landing pages. Started with Non-QM lenders — smaller segment, more receptive to personalized outreach.

How the messaging change resonated

The repositioned website and the demand gen motion reinforced each other. Use-case messaging in LinkedIn ads drove the right lenders to landing pages that spoke their language. Non-QM lenders responded fastest — Prudent AI already had authority in that segment, and the new messaging made the value click immediately instead of requiring a sales call to explain it. QM lenders followed as the ad targeting and landing pages expanded to that segment.

Sales enablement

SLG Prudent AI

The content I built to help sales close faster — battlecards, one-pagers, talktracks, and positioning docs. Right messaging + right tools = shorter sales cycles.

Context

Sales cycle was 100+ days. Reps were improvising on calls — no consistent messaging for QM vs Non-QM buyers, no competitive battlecards, no structured demo flow. Every conversation was a one-off.

The cycle didn't shorten because of one thing. It was three: right messaging that resonated with QM and Non-QM lenders, talktracks that helped reps understand customer needs and communicate product value clearly, and better positioning across every touchpoint.
