About · Kaviyakavi Baskaran

Hi, I'm Kaviyakavi — the PMM who reads customer calls like novels.

I started as a brand marketer writing blogs, drip campaigns, and launching influencer collabs for e-commerce before the influencer economy in India was a thing. Then I made the jump to product marketing at Animaker — a PLG SaaS — and now I'm at Prudent AI running SLG for a mortgage AI co-pilot.

The shift taught me one thing that stuck: putting customers first is the most important thing, no matter what type of campaigns I do. Working closely with customers and the sales team has sharpened that instinct every day I've been a PMM.

01 · Philosophy

Three things I hold
tighter than titles.

PMM is a lot of things. With the AI boom, the list only grows. These are the beliefs I use as a compass when everything else is moving.

01

Strategy over service.

Without strategy, marketing teams become service teams for sales. PMMs belong at the steering end — owning the why behind what gets built, launched, and sold. Not just the execution.

02

Stay rooted in the basics.

The AI boom removed every limit on enablement, automation, and speed. The risk is picking shiny pieces from every room. I stay rooted in industry, market, audience, and customers — strategy flows from there, not from the tool du jour.

03

Audience first. Theory second.

Frameworks are useful until they aren't. What a customer actually said on a sales call will beat a positioning worksheet every time. I translate real language into positioning, messaging, and campaigns that sound like humans because they came from humans.

02 · The longer version

From brand to product,
and what changed.

The short answer: I learned how to listen harder. The longer answer is below.

My first job was writing. Blogs, drip emails, social captions, influencer outreach — the content end of marketing, where you're mostly in service of the brand's voice. It taught me empathy by accident. To write anything readable, I had to picture the person reading it. Not a persona deck. A person.

The jump from brand to product marketing wasn't a new skill — it was the same skill pointed somewhere harder.

At Animaker, I stopped writing around a product and started writing with one. Every onboarding tooltip, every feature landing page, every use-case hero — they all needed to do double duty: tell a story and move a number. That's where the receipts started: +15% adoption, +10% feature use, 3× signups on the pages I owned.

At Prudent AI, the motion flipped. B2B, six-figure deals, long cycles, sales team in the room. The work shifted to positioning the product against a complicated buyer journey, writing talk-tracks and battlecards that AEs actually use, and fixing the funnel end-to-end. The receipts: 3.5× pipeline growth, sales cycle from 100+ to 86 days, +7% on the homepage — all within a few months of joining.

The one thread: in both jobs, the best ideas came from customer calls, sales notes, and support tickets — not brainstorm rooms. I take that seriously. If I'm not in conversations with customers every week, something's wrong with how I'm working.
